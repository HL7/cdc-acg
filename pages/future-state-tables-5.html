4. Assess Evidence a. Evaluate and rate the quality of each individual study using pre-defined quality appraisal criteria for each element. For example:

- Study design and quality of execution

- Internal validity

- External validity (generalizability)

- Precision (i.e., confidence interval)

- Other bias




b. Conduct a Meta-analysis, if needed

c. If evidence derived from non-published study (i.e., patient level data, expert opinion), evaluate and rate the data based on certain criteria. For example:

- Quantitative data: Scale of data, representativeness

- Qualitative: How experts/data identified, representativeness
