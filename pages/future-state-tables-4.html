1. Convene scoping group a. Select multidisciplinary membership for scoping group - include subject SME, guideline methodologist, project manager, INFO SME, systematic reviewer, Health economist, and stakeholders

 Steering Committee (subgroup of steering committee that focuses on scope) Established methods and criteria for membership IOM: Clinical Guidelines

NICE Process

WHO Handbook

Manual for ESHRE guideline development, 2017

The Canadian Guide to Clinical Preventive Health Care

Bridge-Wiz?
2. Get input for the scope of the guideline a. Conduct target user or patient survey to get input

b. Conduct preliminary review to identify key literature. Outputs: a summary of the evidence, a list of key studies and guidelines, and a comparative analysis of relevant guidelines.

c. Research past communications lessons learned

 Systematic Review Group/Evidence Review Group PICOTS input on guideline scope related to:
P - population
I – intervention
C – comparator group
O – outcomes
T – time interval
S – settings
3. Develop draft scope a. Use preliminary assessments to set guideline objectives, select key clinical issues, and develop PICOTS, Logic model and analytical framework.

b. Consider key issues, PICOTS, proposed table of contents and prepare draft scope

- Develop and select key questions

Use structured format, based on the defined PICO components

Determine criteria to prioritize questions

Define outcomes of interest

 Scoping Group Scoping checklist: Established methods and criteria to identify and prioritize key clinical topics

Established structured PICOTS, logic model and analytical framework (draft)
c. Consult with INFORM SME to ascertain informatics scope for key questions- use BRIDGE-Wiz or similar to generate recommendations/scope. Recommendations should define: When? What? Who? Ought? To do What? To Whom? How? And Why? Steering Committee Revised PICOTS, logic model, and analytical framework, and informatics needs
d. Consult with IMPL SME to evaluate plausible clinical workflows impacting options for scope of Intervention and Settings Revised Intervention and Settings (in PICOTS)
4. Consultation a. Convene internal and external stakeholder topic consultations (if needed) to review scoping draft and provide feedback Scoping Group Summary of feedback (use previous completed checklists)
b. Consult with INFORM SME to refine recommendation/scope Scoping Group/Steering Committee Revise.
5. Finalize scope after consultation a. Steering Committee and partner organizations review and approve all drafts.

b. Use scope to determine guideline type (new, interim, update, adaptation etc.)		 Steering Committee, Partners Final PICOTS, logic model, and analytical framework
6. Draft Communications Plan
