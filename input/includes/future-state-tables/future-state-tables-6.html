12.5.0 Future State Tables - Step 6: Formulate (Craft) the Recommendations

What to do (sequence of events) How to do (activities) Responsible entity, Others involved Success Indicators Resources and tools (Samples)
1. Establish policies for writing guideline statements (if not already established by the sponsoring entity) a. Dealing with relationships with industry or other potential sources of bias.

b. Identifying the approach (if any) that is used to select topics for guideline recommendations in addition to evidence review (e.g., list of guideline recommendations in other published guidelines, expert input).

c. Establishing rules (if any) for wording of recommendations (e.g., sentence structure, use or avoidance of specific words).

d. Establishing the process by which specific wording of recommendations will be drafted (e.g., one person followed by comments, group effort).

e. Determining the types of information that must be captured as part of the recommendation crafting process in addition to the recommendations, per se (e.g., rationale for group decisions, benefit vs. harm considerations, patient values/preferences).

f. Having a consistent mechanism for rating the strength (e.g., strong, weak) and direction (e.g., for, against) of recommendations.

g. Prioritizing the importance of recommendations for different levels of point-of-care alerting.

h. Determining the characteristics that will determine the type of alerting process (e.g., hard stop, other modal alert, passive alert, link to reference text, no alert) for each recommendation.

i. Establishing steps by which draft recommendations will be finalized (e.g., voting of members, public comment) as well as voting/comment options (e.g., agree/disagree, agree if modified in strength or wording) and timing (relative to entire guideline text finalization).		 Steering committee;
