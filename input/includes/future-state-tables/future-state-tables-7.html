1. Provide an outline and brief description of the expectations for each section of guideline text that will be needed to support the recommendations, including:

- key sections required in the guideline as a whole (e.g., rationale/need for the guideline, discussion of guideline scope, general information about the search strategy, description of writing group characteristics including relationships with industry, abbreviations, glossary of terms, future research needs)

- required sections under each recommendation (e.g., discussions of benefits, harms and balance of benefits/harms, patient values and preferences in relation to the recommendation, supporting references and/or evidence tables)

- appendices (e.g., detailed tables of evidence with associated text)

 May be established through organization's steering committee for all guidelines or adjusted for each guideline depending on guideline type and rapidity of development

2. Set specific expectations regarding the text development process, for example:

- Aim for succinct text ("less is more")

- Use clear and grammatically correct English with a minimum of jargon

- Use non-text approaches whenever possible to minimize need for text and enable efficient review of summarized information (e.g., bullet points, tables, flow-charts, algorithms)

- Be transparent in specifying and distinguishing fact from opinion

- Reference/cross-link with other guidelines and refer to other guidelines' recommendations as appropriate (i.e., don't re-invent the wheel)

- Avoid scope creep and focus accompanying text on the recommendations rather than peripherally related content

- If indirectly related content does need to be mentioned, give a short conclusion with a few key citations for interested readers

- Keep additional citations to a minimum except those that constitute the evidence supporting the recommendations.

- Avoid citing unstructured/unscientific reviews of the literature
